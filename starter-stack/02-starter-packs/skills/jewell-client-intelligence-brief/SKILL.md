# Jewell client intelligence brief

Synthesise a client's Audience, Brand and Competitor context into one intelligence brief.

## When to use

At the start of an engagement, and again whenever the client intelligence refresh runs (the
monthly cadence) or when a material change in the client's market, offer or competitors is
reported.

## Inputs

- ABC inputs: Audience, Brand, Competitors, gathered from the client directly or from the
  engagement owner.
- Approved source material only: the client's own site, public material, and sources the
  engagement owner has cleared for use.

## Outputs

- A client intelligence brief, filed in that client's `00_Intelligence` folder.

## Steps

1. Gather the ABC inputs already on file for this client. Do not ask the client or the team for
   anything the system already holds.
2. Pull only from approved sources: the client's own material, and sources the engagement owner
   has cleared. Do not use unapproved or unvetted sources.
3. Draft the brief: audience summary, brand summary, competitor summary, and open questions where
   an input is missing.
4. Mark every open question clearly as a gap, not a guess.
5. **Approval gate.** The engagement owner reviews the draft brief, corrects anything wrong, and
   approves it before it is treated as current.

## Guardrails

- Must never use another client's data, brief, or intelligence, even as a reference example.
- Must never treat a guess as a fact. Missing information is flagged as an open question.
- This is a Tier 1 internal output (an intelligence brief, not a client-facing artifact), approved
  by the engagement owner. It only becomes a source for a client-facing output after that
  approval.

## Where the output is filed

That client's `00_Intelligence` folder. One brief per client, kept current, not duplicated.

## Example

Acme Co's engagement owner has audience and brand inputs on file but no competitor list. The
brief drafts the audience and brand sections in full, and lists "competitor set not yet
confirmed" as an open question rather than inventing competitors.

## Success measure

Fewer repeated questions to the client or across the team, because the answer is already in the
brief.
