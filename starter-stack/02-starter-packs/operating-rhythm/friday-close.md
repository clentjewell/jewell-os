# Friday close

The weekly close-out. 30 minutes maximum. Owner: Ronnie. Files to `02_Internal`. See
`OPERATING-SYSTEM.md` Section 12 (weekly rhythm).

Under the revised operating model (constitution rule 1), the OS drafts the close from the week's
Asana, calendar and decision-log activity; Ronnie reviews and corrects it before it is filed.

## Template

```
FRIDAY CLOSE — week of <YYYY-MM-DD>

Owner: Ronnie
Time spent: <target 30 minutes>

1. Wins this week
   -

2. What shipped
   -

3. What slipped, and why
   -

4. Waiting on (blocked on someone else, carrying into next week)
   -

5. Decisions made this week
   - Decision:
     Logged? (yes/no, and where): 
   -

6. Lessons candidates (for weekly washback, not yet approved)
   -

7. Next week's top 3
   1.
   2.
   3.
```

## Notes on filling it in

- **Wins and what shipped** are not the same line. A win can be a relationship moment; "what
  shipped" is a concrete deliverable or milestone.
- **What slipped and why** should name the reason, not just the fact. "Slipped because the
  client's brand assets arrived late" is useful. "Slipped" alone is not.
- **Decisions made this week** must each show whether they were logged to the decision log. An
  unlogged decision is a gap to close before the week is filed, not a detail to skip.
- **Lessons candidates** are not lessons yet. They are raw material for `weekly-washback.md`.
  Nothing here is approved or promoted by writing it down.
- **Next week's top 3** should be genuinely 3 things, not a restated task list. If there are more
  than 3 things that matter, pick the 3 that matter most.

## Filing

Save the completed close to `02_Internal` in Drive, named `YYYY-MM-DD_FridayClose.md` per the
naming convention in `OPERATING-SYSTEM.md` Section 6. It does not need Clent approval to file; it
does need Clent approval before any lesson or decision inside it is promoted to memory or the
playbook, which happens at `weekly-washback.md`.

**Next:** feed the lessons candidates and any open decisions into `weekly-washback.md`.
