# Boundary model

The two boundaries every pack in this kit must respect: the three-layer Jewell × Maxxim model,
and the work/personal boundary. Full detail lives in `../../OPERATING-SYSTEM.md` Sections 2 and
13, and in `../../00-governance/jewell-maxxim-relationship.md` and
`../../00-governance/work-personal-boundary.md`. This file is the one-page operational summary a
pack points back to.

## The three-layer model

- Jewell is human-led: the client relationship, the trust, the judgement, the sign-off.
- Maxxim is system-powered: the engine, the frameworks, the automation, the reusable IP.
- The shared operating layer between them holds the 3D Process, the Deepen loop and the
  client-facing surfaces Jewell runs and Maxxim powers.
- Maxxim must never overpower the Jewell client experience or become the louder brand in front of
  a client.
- Client data must never leak into a reusable Maxxim asset; a Jewell pattern becomes Maxxim IP
  only after review and sanitisation.
- Describe the relationship as: *Jewell, human-led commercialisation, powered by Maxxim.*

## The work/personal boundary

- Work lives in the shared Jewell operating system; personal, legal, family and sensitive matters
  live in a separate, private space.
- The private space is never indexed by the machine, never surfaced to the team, and never in the
  Ask Jewell AI source set.
- The private space is Clent-only.
- The work OS may record the pattern of a personal routine (for example a Monday reset) without
  ever holding its content.
- The Today door may show a single personal reminder line; the content behind it stays private.
- If personal or legal content is ever found in shared Drive, Slack or a prompt, flag it to Clent
  and do not move it yourself.
